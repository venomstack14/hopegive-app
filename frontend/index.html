The system

How we prevent fraud

HopeGive uses a 5-layer verification process with escrow disbursement to ensure every rupee reaches a genuinely needy person.

For applicants

1

Identity verification (KYC)

Aadhaar + PAN verified via DigiLocker API. Biometric liveness check prevents impersonation. One application per verified identity — duplicates auto-rejected and flagged.

2

Document authenticity check

AI model scans every uploaded document for tampering, digital forgeries, and metadata inconsistencies. Medical documents are cross-verified against hospital registration numbers.

3

Field verification & community vouching

Volunteer field agents visit applicants in person for all claims above ₹50,000. For lower amounts, 2 community vouchers from verified neighbours or local officials are required.

4

Need-based scoring & amount cap

An independent review committee assigns a need score (0–100) based on income level, dependants, severity of situation, and existing assets. This score determines the maximum approvable amount — not what the applicant requests.

5

Direct vendor disbursement

Funds are never transferred as cash to the beneficiary. Payments go directly to hospitals, colleges, or suppliers. All transactions are publicly logged on the Transparency page.

Additional safeguards

⏱ 48-hour cooling period

Before any campaign goes live, allowing the system to surface duplicate or suspicious claims.

🚩 Community flagging

Any user can flag a campaign. Three flags auto-pause it for re-review immediately.

🧾 Spending receipts

Beneficiaries must upload vendor invoices within 7 days of disbursement. Unspent funds are refunded to donors.

🔄 18-month re-apply wait

Verified beneficiaries must wait 18 months and prove changed circumstances before reapplying.

🔒 Escrow holding

All donated funds are held in a regulated escrow account and never touched until disbursement is approved.

📋 Legal declaration

All applicants sign a legal declaration that false information is a criminal offence under IPC Section 420.

Public disbursement ledger

Every rupee paid out, who received it, and what it was for — publicly logged and immutable.
